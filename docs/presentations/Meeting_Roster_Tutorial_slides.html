●  03 · The Roster
A working definition
●What it is

A roster is not a guest list. A roster is an attendance ledger.

Each row ties a person to a ticket type, an amount, and a check-in moment — the four pieces of data that, together, answer the only question the Treasurer asks at 9:01 PM: how much money came in the door tonight, and from whom?

The roster is the source of truth for who was in the room and who paid what.

§01 · Three groups

Who ends up on the roster

01Self-registered members.Booked a role. Added by the system. You don't touch.
02Walk-up members.Add manually through the Add Entry form. Choose ticket, amount auto-computes.
03Guests.Always added manually — guests don't have accounts.

●  13 · Cancel & Restore
Two ways to remove a row
Soft Cancel · default

Mark the row cancelled.

The entry stays in the database but is marked Cancelled. The ticket is released, but the row is still visible in the table — greyed out, but visible.

  • Preserves the audit trail
  • Reversible via Restore
  • Use for most cases
Reversible · click Restore on the greyed row.
Hard Delete · advanced

Remove the row entirely.

The row is removed and any role booking they had is released back to the pool. Use this only for duplicates, wrong-meeting mistakes, or urgent role-slot release.

  • Permanent for that meeting
  • Cannot be undone from the UI
  • Ticket payment survives in the Tickets table
Permanent · always prefer soft cancel unless the entry is bogus.
